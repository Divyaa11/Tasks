Surface of Moon

The moon's surface is covered with dead volcanoes, impact craters, and lava flows, some visible to the unaided stargazer. Early scientists thought the dark stretches of the moon might be oceans, and so named such features mare, which is Latin for "seas" .The surface of the Moon is rough, dusty, barren and irregular. It has large craters on its surface and many steep and high mountains.The Moon is coverered with a gently rolling layer of powdery soilwith scattered rocks made from debris blasted out of the Lunar craters by the meteor impacts that created them. Each well-preserved Lunar crater is surrounded by a sheet of ejected material called the ejecta blanket.

Atmosphere of Moon

The Moon has an atmosphere so tenuous as to be nearly vacuum, with three primary gases in the lunar atmosphere neon, helium, and hydrogen, in roughly equal amounts. Small amounts of methane, carbon dioxide, ammonia, and water were also detected. Water vapor has been detected by Chandrayaan-1 and found to vary with latitude, it is possibly generated from the sublimation of water ice in the regolith. The ancient lunar atmosphere was eventually stripped away by solar winds and dissipated into space.
